In HTML, the tag and the tag are both used to apply emphasis to text, but they have different purposes and implications. The tag is used to indicate emphasis or importance of the enclosed text. On the other hand, the tag is primarily used for presentational purposes to apply italic styling to the enclosed text. It doesn't carry any specific semantic meaning or convey importance. The tag simply instructs the browser to render the text in an italic font style. It is commonly used to add visual variety, differentiate a particular word or phrase, or to denote a different language or book title. While both tags can make text italicized, the key difference lies in their intended purposes. The tag is used for semantic emphasis and to indicate importance, while the tag is used for presentational styling and visual differentiation.
